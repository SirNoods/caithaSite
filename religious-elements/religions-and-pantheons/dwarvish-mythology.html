Dwarvish Mythology

Core Beliefs

Religious dwarves believe in honoring their gods through acts that reflect the domains of their chosen deity. Their spirituality is heavily intertwined with daily life, emphasizing craftsmanship, bravery, and the stewardship of nature.

Deities

1. Reigahn – God of Art, Mountains, Mining
- Domains: Art, Mountains, Mining, Stonecraft, Creation
- Symbols: A mountain peak crowned with a forge hammer
- Edicts: Create and uphold structures, both physical and societal, that endure the test of time
- Anathemas: Destroy or deface creations or natural formations without cause
- Favored Weapon: Warhammer
- Abilities: Strength, Wisdom
- Skills: Crafting
- Depicted as a middle-aged dwarf sculpting stone, Reigahn embodies creation and craftsmanship. He is revered as the father of the dwarves and the creator of their mountainous home.
2. Jaevar – Goddess of Battle and Healing
- Domains: War, Healing
- Symbols: A crossed battleaxe and shield
- Edicts: Defend the weak and heal the wounded; fight with honor and courage
- Anathemas: - Shun those in need of help, or kill another Dwarf
- Favored Weapon: Battleaxe
- Abilities: Strength, Charisma
- Skills: Medicine
- Jaevar is the fierce and protective mother of dwarves, depicted in full armor with her battleaxe and shield. She symbolizes the dual nature of battle and healing, embodying strength and care.
3. Odarr – God of Minerals and Gems
- Domains: Minerals, Gems
- Symbols: Two crossed pickaxes
- Edicts: Cherish the wealth of the earth, using it to craft and enrich lives
- Anathemas: Waste or hoard the earth's bounty or exploit it greedily
- Favored Weapon: Pick
- Abilities: Constitution, Intelligence
- Skills: Crafting
- The provider of the earth's riches, Odarr is depicted as a skilled blacksmith. He represents the bounty beneath the earth, urging his followers to use resources wisely and craft them into objects of beauty and utility.
4. Maerun – Goddess of the Skies
- Domains: Sky, Day, Night, Wind and Weather
- Symbols: A cloud partially covering the sun
- Edicts: Protect and respect the harmony of nature's cycles and the weather.
- Anathemas: Disrupt natural cycles or spread chaos through weather manipulation.
- Favored Weapon: Staff
- Abilities: Intelligence, Wisdom
- Skills: Survival
- Maerun controls the skies and the weather, depicted as a majestic dwarf among the clouds. She is a protector of travelers and farmers, guiding them with her wisdom over the cycles of day and night.
5. Rysja – Goddess of Rivers, Seas and Lakes
- Domains: Rivers, Seas, Lakes, Flow of Life
- Symbols: A trio of wavy lines
- Edicts: Preserve and cherish water sources and the life they sustain.
- Anathemas: Pollute or needlessly exhaust water resources
- Favored Weapon: Spear
- Abilities: Constitution, Wisdom
- Skills: Athletics
- Rysja is the life-giver of waters, often shown as a nurturing figure within her aquatic realms. She ensures the flow of life continues, nurturing all that depends on water.
6. Tyre – God of Death
- Domains: Underworld, Death
- Symbols: An upright scythe
- Edicts: Honor the dead through rituals and respect their transition to the afterlife
- Anathemas: Murdering a fellow Dwarf
- Favored Weapon: Scythe
- Abilities: Wisdom, Charisma
- Skills: Underworld Lore
- The solemn guide of souls, Tyre is depicted as a hooded dwarf, ushering the deceased to the afterlife. He comforts those unready to pass and ensures their safe transition, embodying the inevitability and peace of death.
7. Dharohem – God of Gold and Silver
- Domains: - Wealth, Envy
- Symbols: A scale balancing a gold coin and a silver coin
- Edicts: Seek personal wealth to improve oneself
- Anathemas: Let greed lead to your downfall or allow jealousy to guide actions.
- Favored Weapon: Dagger
- Abilities: Dexterity, Charisma
- Skills: Thievery
- Son of Odarr, Dharohem personifies the darker lure of wealth. Depicted with opulent attire, he reminds the dwarves of the thin line between prosperity and greed.

Religious Practices

Dwarves engage in various rituals to honor their gods, from crafting beautiful artifacts as offerings to Reigahn, to solemn rites of passage overseen by Tyre. Their festivals often align with natural cycles, such as solstices or river tides, reflecting their deep connection to the earth's rhythms.

Cultural Impact

The mythology shapes every aspect of dwarvish culture, from their robust architectural styles to their community ethics. Stories of the gods are embedded in every home, taught from a young age, and serve as moral guides and educational tales. These stories not only entertain but also instruct on living a life aligned with dwarvish values.

Creation Myth

In the lore of the dwarves, the world began with the god Reigahn, who first shaped the mountains of Caitha. Not content with mere landscapes, Reigahn infused a stone with the embers of life, casting it into the mountains to create the first dwarves. He charged them with delving into the mountains to unearth and craft its hidden treasures, marking the beginning of dwarvish civilization.
