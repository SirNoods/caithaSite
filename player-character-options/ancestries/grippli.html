Grippli are a reclusive people that have inhabited the eastern swamps of Moordath since the Primordial Era.

Physical Description

Grippli resemble humanoid frogs, and exhibit a remarkable diversity in skin color, ranging from hues of blue and various shades of green to earthy browns and, in rare instances, vibrant oranges. This diversity serves a dual purpose in their swampy habitat. Those with skin colors closely resembling the swamp's natural tones are well-camouflaged, making them adept at stealth and hunting. Conversely, brighter and more vibrant skin tones often signal toxicity, serving as a warning to potential predators.
A grippli grows quickly, reaching adult size of about 2 feet in height about 3 years after hatching, though they're only considered adults around age 12. Gripplis rarely live beyond 60 years, though exceptional individuals occasionally live as long as a century.

Society

Gripplis lead a sophisticated hunter-gatherer lifestyle with which they reshape the landscape to suit their needs: building spillways to trap fish, seeding fruit-bearing trees, sowing cover-granting foliage for future hunts, and more techniques that often escape an agriculturist's eye. These strategies rely on community cooperation as well as dispersed populations, so gripplis commonly live in small villages, each part of a complex web of alliances and relationships. Reclusiveness has preserved grippli lives and lifestyles for millennia, yet they increasingly find themselves threatened by ancient evils and new explorers alike.

Religion

Central to Grippli belief systems is the veneration of the primal forces that sustain their swampy world. They hold deep reverence for "The Everflow," a spiritual embodiment of the ebb and flow of water throughout the marsh. This spirit represents not only life but also death, reflecting the cyclical nature of existence within their unique ecosystem.

Cuisine

Typical foods one would find with Grippli adventurer's are assortments of insects, sometimes grilled, sometimes raw. Over the centuries, Grippli cuisine has become more sophisticated, utilizing grasses like Sedgegrass, as well as various mushrooms. The most notable and unique feature of Grippli cuisine is something that makes it very alien to other people: The Waterweaver's venom. Grippli are immune to it, and just like the spicy taste it gives off to them. To everyone else, ingesting enough of this food can paralyze them.

Names

Grippli names often include resonant vowels and chirped consonants that remain difficult for non-gripplis to vocalize properly. Gripplis that travel widely often adopt one or more names more easily replicated by their associates.

Languages

You can speak, read, and write Common and Grippli, and additional languages equal to your INT modifier (if positive).

Low-Light Vision

You can see in dim light as though it were bright light, and you ignore the concealed condition due to dim light.

Poison Resistance

Your affinity for the natural world grants you protection against some of its dangers. You gain poison resistance equal to half your level, and you gain a +1 status bonus to saving throws against poisons.

Aquatic Adaptation

Your amphibian biology allows you to hold your breath for a long time. You gain the Breath Control general feat as a bonus feat.

History

Primordial Era

In the Primordial Era, the Grippli emerged as one of the earliest sentient beings in Moordath. Their physical resemblance to humanoid frogs, combined with a diverse range of skin colors served them well in the swampy expanses of their homeland. This era laid the groundwork for their sophisticated hunter-gatherer lifestyle, which was intricately tied to the swamp's ecosystem. The Grippli harnessed the landscape to their advantage, building spillways for fishing and sowing vegetation that would later aid in hunting. This period marked the beginning of their deep-seated reverence for "The Everflow," a spiritual force embodying the water's cyclical journey through life and death. While the Grippli stayed as hunter-gatherers for the most part, Lizardfolk developed a civilization. From this arose conflict, while Lizardfolk still live close and in harmony with nature by human standards, the Grippli are of a different opinion.

Era of Storms

The Era of Storms tested the Grippli resilience like nothing before. The cataclysmic Highstorms reshaped the geography of Moordath, turning once-familiar territories into unrecognizable landscapes. Amidst this chaos, the Grippli adaptability and communal spirit shone brightly. Their dispersed villages, each a node in a complex web of alliances, became beacons of stability. The Grippli refined their techniques for living in harmony with the now volatile environment, further cementing their bond with The Everflow. This era also saw the Grippli defending their lands against new threats as ancient evils stirred and explorers encroached upon their territories. The Grippli mastery of stealth and their use of the environment as a defense mechanism ensured their survival through these dark times.

Era of Legends

As the Era of Storms gave way to the Era of Legends, the Grippli faced the dawn of a new age with cautious optimism. Despite their reclusive nature, the challenges of this era pushed some Grippli beyond the boundaries of their swamps. They ventured into the wider world, carrying with them the knowledge of their people and a cuisine utterly alien to other races.

The Grippli involvement in the affairs of the Era of Legends was not without its conflicts. The attempted expansion of other civilizations into their territories tested their resolve. Yet, it was their unyielding spirit and deep-rooted connection to their homeland that saw them through. The Grippli mastery of the landscape, their sophisticated societal structures, and their devout worship of The Everflow enabled them to navigate these challenges. Reluctantly, they share Moordath with the Lizardfolk, as they share the common goal of keeping their land safe.
