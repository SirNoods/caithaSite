Orcs

"In the shadow of storms and legends, the Orcs of Caitha stand tall, their spirits tempered by the trials of the past and their hearts guided by the virtues of honor and resilience."

  • from "A Traveler's Guide to Brurogh", by Casper Mossbane

Traits

Physical Description

Orcs are imposing figures, with muscular builds that speak to their warrior heritage. They typically stand over six feet tall, with green to gray skin that can sometimes carry a bluish hue. Their tusks, emerging from the lower jaw, are a mark of their race, varying in size but always present as a symbol of their strength and ferocity. Their eyes, often gold or red, shine with a depth of wisdom and the fire of their indomitable spirit. They have rough, leathery skin, thick bones and rock hard muscles, making them suited for physically demanding tasks.

Society

Orcs living in areas with many other races are often perceived as somewhat rude or "standoffish," if not hostile, because they neither ask for help nor offer help, even in times of crisis, and are offended if someone tries to help unsolicited. There are rumors that they steal children, because if an Orc finds a child lost in the forest, they will feed it and - if they cannot find the parents or home - raise and integrate it.

Religion

Central to Orcish society is their spirituality, revolving around the worship of Garrosh, a deity of justice and honor. This spirituality guides their daily lives, emphasizing personal growth through adversity and the importance of community responsibility, especially towards children.
Orcish spirituality states that a soul can only grow and become stronger by overcoming the challenges life presents to them. The challenge of supporting or interfering with someone else unsolicited is not only impolite but very hurtful - because you are taking away their chance to grow. Asking for help is something very personal, and doing so could be a way for an Orc to bond or be "too personal/intimate," depending on the existing relationship with the person. Children are an exception - for Orcs, all children are fundamentally the responsibility of the society/community, no matter whose child it is or whether the child is an Orc at all.

Cuisine

Orcish cuisine is hearty, reflecting their hunting and gathering heritage. Dishes often feature game, roots, and wild vegetables, seasoned with the herbs of the Katarisian forests. A common meal might include roasted boar, forest mushrooms, and a brew made from fermented berries.

Names

Orcish names sound powerful and imposing, and are usually bestowed upon them in a ceremony by their parents or caretakers. They don't usually carry last names.

Sample Names:

Garthok, Ertok, Nartha, Gnarg, Yargol, Nakgu, Durbul, Garothmuk

Darkvision

You can see in darkness and dim light just as well as you can see in bright light, though your vision in darkness is in black and white.

History

Primordial Era

The early history of the Orcs is marked by their struggle for survival in a stone-age world. They lived in close-knit nomadic tribes, relying on their strength and cunning to hunt and the wisdom of their elders to navigate the challenges of their environment.

Era of Storms

This period brought catastrophic magical storms that ravaged Caitha, reshaping the land and testing the Orcs like never before. Their resilience during these times was unmatched, as they adapted to the chaos, protecting their communities and growing strong resentments towards the magic that flowed through their world in turmoil. Many tribes started to settle for more permanent homes for better protection.

Era of Legends

After the storms were banished, the Orcs entered a period of rebuilding and growth. It was a time of heroes and legends, where the greatest tales of Orcish valor and wisdom were forged. Societies became more structured and the Orcs' deep respect for the natural world led them to form alliances with other races, sharing their knowledge of survival and spirituality.
