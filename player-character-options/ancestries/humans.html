Humans

Humans are diverse and adaptable people with wide potential and deep ambitions.

Traits

Physical Description

Humans' physical characteristics are as varied as the world's climes. Humans have a wide variety of skin and hair colors, body types, and facial features. Generally speaking, their skin has a darker hue the closer to the equator they or their ancestors lived.
A typical human can live to be around 90 years old. Humans have exceptionally mutable physical characteristics compared to other ancestries, with greater variance in height, weight, and other physical parameters.

Society

Human variety also manifests in terms of their governments, attitudes, and social norms. Though the oldest of human cultures can trace their shared histories thousands of years into the past, when compared to the societies of the elves or dwarves, human civilizations seem in a state of constant flux as empires fragment and new kingdoms subsume the old.

History

Primordial Era

Humans in Caitha developed very similarly to the Kanee or Mossin (Halflings), with small tribes of hunter-gatherers moving through the landscape and relying on natural resources around them for survival. These early humans were nomadic, moving with the seasons to follow game animals and to take advantage of seasonal food sources such as berries, nuts and roots.
Over time, as humans began to develop agriculture and settle in more permanent communities, their culture became more complex. In the Mediterranean climate of Caitha, early human settlements were focused around the cultivation of olives, grapes and wheat, which allowed for the development of trade networks and the establishment of larger, more organized communities.

As these settlements grew, they began to develop their own unique cultures and social structures. Some became more egalitarian, with power shared among the members of the community, while others developed hierarchical systems with chiefs or kings at the top. Religion also played a significant role in many early human societies, with deities worshipped for their ability to bring fertility to the land, protect the community from harm or guide human actions.

Era of Storms

As the human-caused Highstorms swept across Caitha, civilizations faced unprecedented trials. This era, characterized by its relentless, catastrophic weather, brought about a significant reshaping of human societies. Cities crumbled, coastlines changed, and entire regions were rendered unrecognizable, forcing humans to either flee to safer grounds or innovate ways to survive.

Human adaptability was crucial during these times. They built fortified settlements, adapted agriculture to volatile climates, and formed new alliances, sometimes with other species, to enhance their chances of survival. The storms, while destructive, also served as a catalyst for technological and magical advancements. Humans, often at the forefront of innovation, pioneered developments in storm prediction and weatherproof architecture.

Communities that once might have warred with each other found common ground, united by the need to survive. New leaderships emerged, often marked by a more collective, survival-focused mentality rather than the traditional hierarchies of the past.

Era of Legends

With the eventual abatement of the Highstorms, confined now to the outer edges of the known world, humans entered what would be known as the Era of Legendsâ€”a time of rebuilding and exploration, but also a time of new imperialist wars. Freed from the constant threat of stormy destruction, human societies flourished and expanded, both in population and cultural influence.

The new era brought about a renaissance of arts, culture, and science as humans explored not only their changed world but also the realms of magic and technology made possible through their innovations during the storms. Cities were rebuilt with grander visions, incorporating both the aesthetic and practical technologies developed during the darker times.

Human explorers set out to rediscover lost lands, establish new trade routes, and exchange knowledge with other species whose capabilities and technologies had also been shaped by the storms. Diplomacy flourished as humans sought to solidify their place in a world that was increasingly interconnected.

Meanwhile, scholars and mystics delved deeper into the understanding of magic, particularly that which had been influenced by the storms' arcane energies. This pursuit of knowledge led to a cultural golden age, where human universities and libraries became the centres of learning for all races of Caitha.
