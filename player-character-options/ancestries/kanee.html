Kanee

The Kanee or Kanei (sometimes referred to as Night-Elves or Dark-Elves, even though they are not related to elves) are a race inhabiting parts of the northern tundras of Kataris.
They emerged as a sapient species from the Kanrak, who had adapted to survive the harsh and icy environment of the northern tundras.

Traits

Physical Description

Kanee are a stout and resilient race, evolved from the rugged Kanrak of the harsh tundra. Standing shorter than the average human but with broad, muscular frames, Kanee possess remarkable strength and endurance. Their most distinctive features are their large, curved horns, which serve not only as symbols of their heritage but also as canvases for their cultural expressions. The Kanee’s eyes are another unique trait; capable of turning entirely black, these eyes enhance their night vision, a mysterious adaptation from their evolutionary predecessors. Their skin tones range from deep dark blue to lighter shades, often accented by the vibrant, painted horns that tell stories of lineage and personal achievement.

Society

Religion

The predominant religion among the Kanee is the worship of Roor, the deity symbolizing fire, life, warmth, and light. Known as the Followers of the Flame, this faith centers around Roor, who is revered not only as a creator and sustainer but also as a destroyer who can wield fire to protect and to punish. Roor is depicted as a towering figure enveloped in flames, representing the eternal and life-giving warmth that sustains the Kanee through the harsh tundra winters.

Cuisine

Kanee cuisine reflects their tundran herbivore origins, and is not particularly exciting compared to other cuisines of the world. It consists of stew and bread, berries, mushrooms and lichen, and they can digest many different plants.

Traditions

Horn-painting

Horn painting is a long standing tradition in Kanee culture, daring back to the earliest days of their sapience. The Kanee believe that their horns, which are a prominent feature of their physiology, are a symbol of their ancestral roots and connection to their land. Painting their horns is a way for them to express this connection and celebrate their heritage.
The process of horn-painting involves using natural dyes made from crushed berries, lichens and other plant materials to create intricate designs on the horns. The designs often include abstract images of animals, landscapes, and other symbols meaningful to the Kanee.
Horn-painting is typically done during important cultural events, such as the coming-of-age ceremony for young Kanee. The designs are meant to be a reflection of the individual, as well as their connection to their community and the natural world. Over time, this practive has evolved into a complex art form, with skilled artisans creating intricate and detailed designs that are admired and sought after by everyone. It has become an important part of Kanee culture, helping to preserve their traditions and connect them to their past.

Ancestral worship

Ancestral worship is an important aspect of Kanee culture, and is deeply intertwined with their reverence for the natural world. The Kanee believe that the spirits of their ancestors continue to exist in the world, and can offer guidance. To honor and maintain a connection with their ancestors, the Kanee often keep the horns of their dead and make them into ceremonial horns.
The horns are believed to contain the essence of the ancestor's soul, and by blowing into the horn, the living Kanee can communicate with and pay respects to their departed loved ones. This practice is particularly important during ceremonies and rituals, such as births, coming of age and funerals.
The ancient practive of hornpainting reinforced this type of worship, as it better preserves the memory of the deceased and acknowledges the impact they had on the world during their lifetime.

History

Primordial Era

Early Kanee culture was based on nomadic tribes that followed herds of migratory animals for food and resources. Scholars are not sure how they discovered fire in the tundra, but it was likely by accident, through a natural wildfire caused by powerful lighting strikes. They quickly learned how to control and use fire for warmth, cooking and protection against predators. Fire was seen as a valuable resource and played a significant role in early Kanee culture, with many myths and legends featuring it prominently.
As Kanee society developed and expanded, they began to settle in permanent locations and developed agriculture and animal husbandry. This allowed for larger populations to thrive, and more complex societies to emerge.
As the Kanee became more advanced, they began to keep written records of their history, culture, and achievements. The first writings emerged around 2000 years ago, and marked the beginning of a new era of Kanee civilization.

Era of Storms

These early writings documented the rise and fall of the first Kanee kingdoms around Khalan, Joskja and Ivarja, while the Highstorms were ravaging the world, which were characterized by central governments, organized military and elaborate public works. A time of expansion began for the Kanee, as they discovered seafaring and were the first to arrive to the island chain in the northwest of Kataris and at the north pole of Caitha. Having a natural physiological advantage over other races in the cold, they had almost no competition with settling and expanding there.

Era of Legends

Following the tumultuous Era of Storms, the Kanee found themselves in a world reshaped by elemental forces. With the stabilization of the Highstorms at the world’s edge, the Kanee entered the Era of Legends, a time when they could finally turn from mere survival to the restoration and advancement of their culture.
