Dwarves

The Dwarves are an ancestry that shares a common ancestor with Humans and Gnomes, and that has inhabited the mountainous regions of the world for a long while. They have long been and to this day still are largely organised in Clans, a substitute for families.

Traits

Physical Description

Dwarves have a short and stocky build, standing shorter than humans, with wide, compact bodies and burly frames. Both male and female dwarves have evolved to grow thick bears that can act as a filter for the rough and dusty air deep within the mountains. Dwarves can live up to around 350 years old, and is considered an adult at the age of 50.

Society

The murder of one dwarf by another is seen as the most atrocioous and terrible crime a dwarf can commit. For this reason, there is no death sentence among Dwarvenkind. Instead, murderers are exiled.

Clans

Dwarves don't have classic family constructs, and their last names are the names of their clans. Ofte times, within a clan, many dwarves are already related, so dwarves marry and migrate to other clans if they so please. When two dwarves have a child, it is assigned a so-called protector from their clan, because it is believed to be unhealthy for a child to only ever be raised by their own parents. The protector then takes on a lot of the work that comes with raising said child. A clan's bond is very close, and humans often compare them to large families.

Religion

Dwarves believe in a pantheon of gods and have a rich mythology surrounding them.
In larger cities, priests are usually appointed by the priestly order of their patron god to oversee places of worship. Core values that can be found with any god are good work, and the preservation of ancient dwarvish history.

Cuisine

Typical foods one would find in dwarven establishments are Ironroot, goat cheese and bread, the staples of dwarven food. Living in the mountains, goats are the natural livestock dwarves have domesticated, so goat milk, meat and cheese are the most common ingredients in their cuisine. Ironroot, a native plant to the mountains, chicory, wheat grass and mushrooms even grow underground, and compliment their foods fabulously.

Names

Dwarves typically introduce themselves by their first name and clan, followed by any titles or honorifics they might have. Their names usually contain hard consonants and typically are 2-3 syllables long.

The 50th Birthday

For dwarves, the 50th birthday holds an important meaning. After fifty years, they cross the boundary to adulthood. While of course every day worth celebrating has a big feast, what makes the 50th special is the ritual that is performed. Every child gets a special, hand-made metal trinket by their protector at birth. At the ritual to adulthood, both the protector and the now adult dwarf will throw their trinkets into a forge, melting the metals down once again to mark the end of their bond, and the beginning of the adult life of the dwarf. The molten metal from the trinket is typically cast into an ingot, and treasured until the young dwarf becomes a protector themselves.

The Duel

When dwarves have a disagreement they can not or don't want to settle with words, it results in a duel. Now, a duel must be agreed upon by both fighting parties, and a third neutral one. They will agree on weapons, from fists in unarmed combat to axes and hammers, and how long will be fought. This can be from drawing first blood, from knocking someone unconscious or just off their feet, until someone gives up or even dies.
This is the only legal way for a dwarf to kill another dwarf, but of course it always comes with the risk of being killed yourself.
Duels happen fairly often, though normally they don't go all the way until someone dies. They are fought to settle disagreements, to make out a new leader or find a successor, or just to blow off some steam.

History

Primordial Era

In their early history, Dwarves have not yet discovered fire, and they relied on bioluminescent fungi like Glimmercap and other natural light sources to illuminate their tunnels and caves. They also relied on hunting and gathering for their sustenance, although hunting was the more successful way of maintaining their diet. Eventually, they discovered fire by striking flint against rocks, and it quickly became a valuable resource for warmth, food and metalworking.
With readily available materials all around them, Dwarves became excellent miners, stoneworkers and smiths. As time went on, they carved more and more elaborate homes into the mountainsides and cliff faces. This is also the time, where their first writings emerged.
With the discovery of fire, metalworking and the trade among tribes, Dwarves formed a large community and complex social structure, with rulers and a system of laws. They also established a writing system, at first occurring only on stone tablets, and on their walls.

Era of Storms

The ancient city

The ancient city of Hrotmaerahn (translated literally to "Old Sky Mountain") was the earliest city to emerge from Dwarvenkind. During the Era of Storms, Hrotmaerahn began to grow rapidly in population as a bastion against the Highstorms that plagued the surface.
The exact founding date of Hrotmaerahn has been lost to time. As it grew, Dwarves began to develop trade relationships outside of the city, with other Dwarven cultures and settlements, exchanging knowledge, valuable metals and Stoneworks for goods and resources. With the discovery of Moonstone, a gemstone that collects and keeps the moons light within for a considerable amount of time, their advancements into the depths of mountains went even further.

Discovery of Runebinding

According to Dwarven legend, the discovery of Runebinding was a fortunate accident. It is said that a group of Dwarves were exploring the depths of a mountain cave near Hrotmaerahn when they came across a peculiar set of glowing symbols etched into the stone. Not knowing what they were, the Dwarves touched the symbols and were immediately infused with magical energy.
Excited by this newfound power, the Dwarves began to experiment with the symbols, carving them into stone and metal and imbuing them with different magical properties. Over time, they discovered that by combining different runes and binding them to anchors, they could create powerful spells that allowed them to do many things like teleporting, or creating powerful wards and enchantments. Yet another growth spurt in the development and history of the Dwarves.
As their mastery of Runebinding grew, so too did their reputation as master craftsmen. They began to craft magical artifacts of immense power.
