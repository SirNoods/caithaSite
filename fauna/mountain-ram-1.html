Physical Characteristics

Mountain Rams are robust, quadrupedal creatures with impressive physique and a majestic air. Their sturdy bodies are covered in dense, coarse fur, which serves as insulation against the biting mountain winds. They sport formidable, spiral-shaped horns that arc from their foreheads, the tips often sharpened naturally through rigorous mountain-climbing and butting contests. These powerful creatures stand about five feet tall at the shoulder and can grow up to nine feet in length, from nose to tail.

Habitat

Mountain Rams are exclusively found in the high-altitude regions of Caitha, primarily inhabiting the lofty crags and precipices of the formidable mountain ranges. Their remarkable adaptation to these extreme environments allows them to traverse near-vertical cliffs and precipitous slopes with ease.

Behaviour and Lifestyle

These creatures are known for their sure-footedness and agility, allowing them to navigate treacherous terrains with grace. They often form tight-knit herds led by a dominant male, while the females, known as ewes, protect the young. Mountain Rams are known for their playful butting contests, a way to establish dominance within the herd.

Diet

Their diet mainly consists of hardy mountain vegetation such as lichens, mosses, Ironroot, and tough alpine shrubs. They also consume mineral-rich stones, which not only provide essential nutrients but also aid in the grinding down of their ever-growing horns.

Reproduction

Breeding typically occurs during the spring when the high-altitude meadows are carpeted with fresh vegetation. After a gestation period of about six months, the ewes give birth to a single lamb, which is guarded diligently within the protective confines of the herd.

Cultural Significance

Mountain Rams hold deep cultural significance for the Dwarves of Caitha. They are regarded as symbols of endurance, resilience, and the indomitable spirit of the mountain dwellers. The dwarves have adopted the Mountain Rams as trusted companions, employing them for transportation through the rugged terrain and even into battle.

Domestication

Dwarves have harnessed the strength and agility of Mountain Rams for centuries, using them as formidable mounts in both times of war and peaceful journeys through the treacherous mountains. These highly domesticated creatures have become an integral part of Dwarven culture, providing not only transportation but a deep emotional connection.

Threats

While Mountain Rams are formidable and well-equipped to defend themselves, they do face threats in their alpine homes. Fearsome predators like the Frostwyrm and Mountaindragon occasionally target them, seeking to make a meal of these hardy herbivores. Dwarves have learned to safeguard their prized steeds, often embarking on perilous missions to eliminate these threats and ensure the continued existence of their beloved Mountain Rams.
